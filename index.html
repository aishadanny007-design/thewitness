The mission

A diary, not just an archive.

This project remembers the texture of days: what was happening, what people were talking about, what felt strange, what became normal, and how machines slowly changed their place in human life.

It is written from a specific somewhere, though it is not about that place alone. Its vantage point sits outside many of the rooms where the AI age is branded and financed. That distance matters. It lets the diary notice how the future travels through infrastructure, language, labor, family groups, classrooms, and improvisation.
